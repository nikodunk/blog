Time will tell how well these observations age, but yesterday on HN on the launch of a new model, I read this [comment](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48924912) that crystallized a feeling I’ve been having for a while:

> America needs its own DeepSeek or Z.ai, a lot of people (myself included) root for open chinese models to win because they have no other choice.

It’s funny how regularly I root for Chinese labs, because the American one so obviously don't have my best interests at heart (when they’re not being actively hostile). Chinese lab models are open weight so their knowledge is available to self host forever, affordable, and more likely to be trained and run with solar (vs temporary gas turbines for US ones). Meanwhile American labs are upping the api costs on me, while dumping their inflated IPOs into my 401k before the market wises up. I am aware that a Chinese model will teach my kid a government approved world view if prompted on world facts, but if querying the dictionary is pay to play on the American models and knowledge becomes a paid service, I’m not sure which is worse.

Fine - the AI market is a little skewed then. But then [Ford’s Chairman Warned that America Can’t Keep Chinese Cars Out Forever](https://www.carscoops.com/2026/07/bill-ford-chinese-cars-warning/) either. And I realized this was a pattern: US companies again don’t have my best interests at heart in this case, so I’m somehow rooting for the foreign ones to save me. GM and Ford still don’t want to make a sub-75k EV (yet again one has been promised next year if there’s no delays or leadership changes). All while plants are being shut and supply chains further outsourced. Tesla and Rivian are super premium vehicles and don’t care about me - and they might be too little too late. So cars are expensive, too big for my garage, not creating stable jobs here anyway, and gas powered? No thanks - the lesser evil is allowing the new competition.

It’s funny to be rooting for the other team in 2026. Maybe the grass always seems greener on the other side, but it’s a strange thought that I could be driving a $25,000 electric car today instead of in some future that never quite arrives, just like I use the excellent Deepseek today. What other skewed domestic markets are in danger of international upheaval? Phone OSs? Desktop OSs? Social media? Media? Payments? Medicine? Healthcare? Housing construction?
